Joe Rogan

Joe Rogan is a controversial character for some, he's a Comedian, podcast host, and a UFC commentator. He's an incredibly interesting person, I have listened to hundreds of hours of his podcast you really kind of get a feel for who he is, and I have never been boerd with the conversation. Joe always keeps and open mind and really enjoys a challenge, he is very honest with everyone and lives a life that makes him happy and keeps his family happy. Joe has not only inspired me but hundreds if not thousands of other people to get out and try new things, he believes you only have one life and you have to live it to the fullest. I respect him and he's an inspiration to me, because he makes a living off of just being a nice, humble, and driven individual that you can't help but to admire.

Cameron Hanes

Cam Hanes is the most successful bow hunter in the world, and a very successful ultrarunner as well as a family man with a day job. He runs ultra marathons notably running 102 miles in 24 hours, he runs a marathon a day before he goes to his 9-5 job. Cam still finds time for his family and practicing shots with his bow as well as lifting weights. After running and work he goes home to his family where he lifts weights and practices with his bow, his real passion is hunting. He hasn't had an unsucessful hunt since 2009, which is what makes him the most successful bow hunter in the world. I respect him, because his drive is almost inhuman, he strives to be the best in anything he does, this quality is inspirational for thousands of people across the globe including me. I have taken up bow hunting and running due to Cam Hanes, and I love every second of it.

David Goggins

David Goggins is an ex Navy Seal, ultrarunner and challenges himself as much as he can daily to push past his previous limits. He is an extremely driven and motivated indivdual who can make even the most disciplined and active people feel like they're not doing enough. Hes won a multitude of marathons and ultra marathons, as well as writing a book and adding constant new challenges to his day to day routine. He also has accomplished so much in life, while over coming so many challanges such as a terrible childhood envrioment, heart surgery, and being overweight for a long time. I respect him, because he's extremely motivated and driven and when you listen to him speak you can't help but feel some of his drive and determination rub off on you.

Bernie Sanders

People call him a radical, a socialist, and a kook, but through all the adversity and the brigades of insults and hurdles thrown his way he's never stopped fighting for the American people. Bernie has been fighting for the people of the nation long before I was even born, protesting segregation, gay rights, corporate greed, and social programs for the common people. Sanders wants the best for his nation, he understands the struggles of some of the poorest people in the nation and he doesn't believe or current system fights for these people, instead it rewards the top 1% of wealth. I hope that he wins the presidency this upcoming election, but I do of course have my doubts. I think his policies will greatly improve day to day life for the average citizen, I really hope some of them can come to fruition and we can move towards where the rest of the world is already at as far as social programs and climate change are concerned. I respect Bernie Sanders for standing up for the small guy, and for what he believes in, as well as never giving up in his long battle for the people of our nation.
